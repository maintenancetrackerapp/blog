Rental Property Seasonal Maintenance Checklist (All 4 Seasons)

Stay ahead of breakdowns and costly repairs with a simple quarterly maintenance routine. Print this out and work through it every season.

7 min read  ·  Updated March 2026

The most expensive repairs landlords face are almost never surprises — they're the result of deferred maintenance. A $150 furnace tune-up prevents a $3,000 emergency replacement. A $40 gutter cleaning prevents $8,000 in water damage. Preventive maintenance is simply the cheapest form of property management.

This checklist breaks down what to inspect and service at your rental property each season. Work through it once a quarter, log what you find, and you'll spend less money, have fewer emergencies, and keep tenants happier over the long run.

How to use this checklist: Schedule a property walk-through at the start of each season. Bring this list, a phone for photos, and your maintenance log. Record everything you find — not just problems, but confirmation that items were checked and found okay. That record protects you too.

🌿

Spring Checklist

Spring is your recovery season — assessing winter damage and preparing for warmer months. It's the best time to catch exterior issues before they worsen through summer storms.

Exterior

  • Inspect roof for winter damage — missing shingles, damaged flashing, ice dam residue
  • Clean and inspect gutters and downspouts; ensure drainage away from foundation
  • Check exterior caulking around windows and doors; re-apply where cracked or missing
  • Inspect foundation for new cracks or water infiltration signs
  • Check driveway and walkways for frost heave damage; repair trip hazards
  • Inspect deck, porch, and railings for winter wear; tighten loose fasteners

HVAC

  • Schedule AC tune-up before first hot days (book early — HVAC companies fill up fast in May)
  • Replace HVAC filter
  • Clean AC condenser unit (clear leaves and debris from around the unit)
  • Test AC operation before temperatures rise

Plumbing and Interior

  • Test all smoke and CO detectors; replace batteries
  • Check under sinks for any slow leaks that developed over winter
  • Inspect water heater — look for rust or corrosion
  • Check sump pump operation if applicable
☀️

Summer Checklist

Summer is your window for exterior projects and your highest-risk season for AC failures. Address outdoor issues and make sure cooling systems are running well before peak heat.

  • Touch up exterior paint where peeling or faded
  • Inspect and repair window screens
  • Treat deck or wood surfaces if due for staining/sealing
  • Check landscaping — ensure trees and shrubs aren't encroaching on the structure
  • Inspect and clean dryer vent (fire hazard if blocked)
  • Replace HVAC filter (mid-summer if running heavily)
  • Check attic ventilation — adequate airflow reduces cooling costs and prevents moisture
  • Inspect basement or crawl space for moisture or mold signs
  • Test GFCI outlets in kitchens, bathrooms, and exterior locations
🍂

Fall Checklist

Fall is your most important season for proactive maintenance. Everything you do now directly prevents your most expensive winter emergencies. Don't skip this one.

Heating System

  • Schedule furnace inspection and tune-up before cold weather hits
  • Replace HVAC filter
  • Bleed radiators if the property has a hot water heating system
  • Test thermostat operation; replace batteries in programmable thermostats
  • Check that all heating registers and vents are unobstructed

Winterization

  • Shut off and drain exterior hose bibs
  • Inspect and seal gaps around pipes where they enter the building
  • Check weatherstripping on all exterior doors; replace if worn
  • Inspect attic insulation; add if inadequate (prevents ice dams)

Exterior

  • Clean gutters after leaves fall — this is critical
  • Trim tree branches away from the roof and power lines
  • Inspect and caulk around all exterior penetrations (pipes, wires, vents)
  • Check chimney if property has a fireplace — sweep if used
  • Test smoke and CO detectors; replace batteries
❄️

Winter Checklist

Winter maintenance is mostly about monitoring and rapid response. Your goal is to catch problems before they become emergencies — frozen pipes and ice dams are the big ones.

  • Check on the property after major storms — inspect for ice dams, roof stress, water infiltration
  • Ensure heating system is functioning (confirm with tenant mid-winter)
  • Replace HVAC filter
  • Monitor for signs of pipe freezing in unheated spaces (garages, crawl spaces)
  • Check that attic hatch is insulated and sealed (significant heat loss point)
  • Inspect basement for moisture from snowmelt
  • Ensure adequate heat is maintained if property is vacant (minimum 55°F)

The Bottom Line

Seasonal maintenance isn't exciting — but it's one of the highest-ROI activities a landlord can do. A few hours each quarter, a consistent checklist, and the discipline to log what you find will save you thousands over the life of your rental property and keep your tenants considerably happier along the way.

Bookmark this page and come back each season. Better yet, put a recurring calendar reminder on the first day of each new season so you never let it slip.
